The Problem

Most cybersecurity training does not make people job-ready.

Many entry-level programmes explain terminology but stop short of showing how GRC operates in practice. Learners finish with theory, but little confidence in risk, audit, ISO 27001, or real deliverables.

Too theoretical

Concepts are introduced without enough exposure to how security governance functions inside a real organisation.

No real artefacts

Learners finish without producing a risk register, SoA, policy, or audit outputs they can discuss credibly.

Weak interview readiness

Without structured outputs and applied thinking, it is difficult to show employers practical capability.

The Approach

A practical, role-aligned route into GRC.

GRC Analyst Launchpad is designed around the responsibilities of a junior GRC analyst. The focus is not passive learning, but producing work that reflects how governance, risk, and compliance are carried out.

What you will learn to do

  • Identify and articulate risks in business terms
  • Build and maintain a structured risk register
  • Understand ISO 27001 and ISMS fundamentals
  • Map risks to appropriate controls
  • Assess vendor risk and due diligence gaps
  • Review audit evidence and write findings

Roles this prepares you for

  • GRC Analyst
  • Information Security Analyst
  • Risk & Compliance Analyst
  • ISMS Support / ISO 27001 Support roles
  • Audit and assurance support roles

Portfolio Outcomes

What you will build.

Every major stage contributes to professional artefacts designed for interview discussion, portfolio use, and practical confidence.

Risk Register

Structured risks, scoring, owners, controls, and treatment decisions.

Statement of Applicability

Relevant control selection, implementation status, and justification.

Security Policy

A concise, professional policy aligned to organisational need.

Audit Findings

Clear observations, business risk, and actionable recommendations.

Curriculum

Programme structure.

The curriculum moves from core concepts to practical delivery using a consistent fintech case study throughout.

Week 1

Foundations of GRC & Risk
Core concepts, terminology, and structured risk statements.

Week 2

Threats, Breaches & Control Failures
Understanding incidents, control failure, and business impact.

Week 3

ISO 27001 & ISMS
Practical understanding of how organisations structure security.

Week 4

Controls, Policies & SoA
Connecting risks to controls and building a Statement of Applicability.

Week 5

Risk Register & Assessment
Developing one of the most important artefacts in GRC.

Week 6

Third-Party Risk Management
Assessing vendors and making informed risk decisions.

Week 7

Audit & Evidence
Understanding control effectiveness, audit evidence, and findings.

Week 8

Capstone Project
Delivering a full GRC assessment for FinSure Payments Ltd.

Frequently Asked Questions

No. The programme is designed to be accessible to beginners, graduates, and career switchers.

No. ISO 27001 is covered, but the programme is broader and supports entry-level GRC, risk, audit, and security governance roles.

Will I receive templates and practical materials?

Yes. Templates are included so you can produce structured, professional outputs throughout the programme.

Can I use the outputs in interviews?

Yes. The programme is designed to help you discuss real artefacts and demonstrate practical understanding to employers.
